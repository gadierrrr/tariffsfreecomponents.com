# Supplier Sourcing Playbook

This database is designed to become a supplier acquisition and verification pipeline for TariffsFreeComponents.com. Treat every entry as a candidate until product-level documentation is collected.

## Launch Categories

Prioritize categories with clear tariff sensitivity and repeat buyer demand:

- Connectors, cable assemblies, and wire harnesses
- Fasteners and specialty hardware
- CNC machined parts and precision components
- Valves, fittings, and fluid-control components
- PCB assemblies and electronics manufacturing services
- Electrical enclosures, HVAC controls, bearings, and motion components

## Supplier Sources

Use these channels to expand the database:

- Thomasnet category pages for U.S. and Canada supplier discovery
- NIST MEP Supplier Scouting for domestic manufacturing leads
- SBA Onshoring Portal for verified U.S. manufacturer discovery
- USITC DataWeb to identify high-volume HTS categories and country-of-origin shifts
- Panjiva, ImportGenius, and TradeAtlas for trade-data-driven supplier discovery
- Industry associations, trade shows, and distributor line cards

## Qualification Rules

Do not mark a supplier as verified based on a homepage or directory listing. Verification requires product-level evidence.

Required before a premium compliance badge:

- Product or product-family HTS code
- Country of origin by SKU or product family
- Manufacturing location
- Certificate of origin or equivalent support
- Relevant certifications, if the category requires them
- Confirmation that the supplier wants RFQs for that category

## Outreach Sequence

1. Send a founding supplier email focused on buyer demand and RFQ access.
2. Ask for the highest-demand tariff-advantaged product families first.
3. Offer a free basic listing for qualified suppliers.
4. Upsell verified placement after documentation is received.
5. Reserve premium placement for suppliers with strong documentation and fast RFQ response.

Would you be the right person to confirm which product families you want listed for RFQs?

Best,
TariffsFreeComponents.com
